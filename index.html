About Payments

Kinfolks does not hold, collect, move, or take any money.

We are a planning and management hub — an app to keep your trips, dinners, nights out, and events organized in one place. That's it.

All actual money goes through the PayPal or Venmo set up by the host of each event. Those services have their own terms. The host of the event is fully in charge of their PayPal/Venmo and the group funds collected there.

Kinfolks takes no cut, no fees, and has no access to anyone's funds. Always make sure you're sending to the right person before you pay.

Refunds: If someone opts out, getting money back is between them and the host — the app can't refund anyone. The general rule: opt out before the due date and you can ask the host for a full refund; after that, the money may already be spent, so refunds aren't guaranteed. The "Request Refund" button just messages the host; they send it back through Venmo/PayPal.

About the Vault & Passcode

The Vault is just a scoreboard — a running tally of money collected and spent for this event. It does not hold any real money.

The passcode is only for logging spending — so not just anyone can type in "spent $40 on gas." It keeps the record accurate. It has nothing to do with withdrawing money, because there's no money in the app to withdraw.

Where the real money is: everyone pays the host directly through the host's Venmo/PayPal. That money sits in the host's own account — it's already theirs. The host spends it on the event (cabin, gas, food) straight from their account. No withdrawal, no code, no app involvement with the actual cash.

Think of it like a group spreadsheet: it tracks who paid and what got spent, while the host holds and spends the real money.

How to Collect Money as the Host

Quick note: PayPal and Venmo don't have a "pool." You just share your own link or username — everyone sends to it, and it all lands in your account. Here's how to set yours up.
💙 PayPal (PayPal.Me link)
1. Open the PayPal app (or paypal.com) and sign in — or make a free account.
2. Tap your profile picture, then look for "PayPal.Me" (or go to paypal.com/paypalme).
3. Pick your link name — like paypal.me/alexwilliams. This is the part you'll paste into Kinfolks.
4. Back in Kinfolks, choose PayPal and type just the name after the slash (e.g. alexwilliams).
5. Done! When people tap "Pay" they'll go to your link, type the amount, and send it to you.
🟦 Venmo (your username)
1. Open the Venmo app and sign in — or make a free account.
2. Tap the menu (☰) or your profile to find your username — it starts with an @ (like @Alex-Williams-7).
3. In Kinfolks, choose Venmo and type your username without the @ (e.g. Alex-Williams-7).
4. Done! "Pay" sends people to your Venmo so they can send their share.
Tip: In Venmo settings you can set payments to "Private" so the whole world doesn't see them.
A few host tips:
• Tell people exactly what to put in the note (like "Cabin trip - your name").
• Set the event's "Money Due By" date and match it when you remind people.
• The money goes to you — you're in charge of it, including any refunds. Kinfolks just tracks who's paid.
